### Era J: Creative Co-Pilot and Release Decision (Engine-Assisted, Human-Decided)

Added 2026-06-08. Era I makes the *mechanical/production* surface fully loop-produced and verified. Era J addresses the surface the loop can only *assist*, never replace: the creative judgment (is it fun, does the theme have a soul), balance taste, and the release go/no-go. The research-backed reality is that fun is *emergent* (only confirmable by human play) and demand cannot be manufactured. Era J therefore does not try to automate those decisions; it builds the tooling that shrinks the human surface to its irreducible core — *select, feel, approve* — and makes that core fast and well-informed.

**Guiding principle for Era J.** Turn creation from *writing* into *choosing*: the engine generates many candidates and verifies the mechanical layer; the human curates, judges fun, and signs off. The fun verdict is a first-class, human-in-the-loop gate — not an automated metric. North-star, extended: `… × **creative leverage**` — output per human decision, with the human-judgment boundary preserved.

**Boundaries (all prior boundaries unchanged; Era J additions in bold):**
- **The fun/feel verdict is permanently human and cannot be automated (emergence); the engine provides the playtest harness and the gate, never the judgment.**
- **The release go/no-go, creative/taste selection, and theme "soul" remain human decisions; the engine generates candidates and evidence only.**
- **Market demand (wishlists, discoverability, UA) and the human Steam business/legal steps are Ring 3 and out of scope.**
- Rust/local owns generation, verification, and provenance; surfaces read-only; conservative wording.

#### Milestone 57: Candidate Generation and Curation Cockpit
Goal: make creation a curation act — generate many variants, let a human pick.
Target deliverables: N-variant candidate generation (cards, tuning, flavor, store copy) reusing the Milestone 30 generation path; a read-only curation surface where human selection is recorded as provenance; a demo and Scenario Coverage v51.
Success criteria: a human can select among generated candidates; selections are provenance-tracked; generation never performs a trusted write.

#### Milestone 58: Human Playtest Harness and Fun-Feel Gate (Design-Gate-First)
Goal: give an honest, first-class answer to "is it fun" — a human-judged gate, since fun is emergent and un-automatable.
Target deliverables: a design-gate ADR establishing that the fun verdict is human-in-the-loop (not an automated metric); a structured human-playtest capture (first-session signals such as "one more run?", a retention proxy, and feedback); a fun-feel evaluation gate that records a human verdict and blocks promotion until human sign-off; a demo and Scenario Coverage v52.
Success criteria: a playtest session is captured structurally; a title cannot be marked release-ready without a human fun verdict; the gate is descriptive of *who decided*, never an automated fun score.

#### Milestone 59: Narrative and Theme-Arc Authoring Assist
Goal: assist the "soul" — generate candidate narrative/theme material for human selection.
Target deliverables: phase-shift narrative / theme-wit / flavor candidate generation extending the Milestone 39 narrative system; human-curated selection and integration (read-only assist); a demo and Scenario Coverage v53.
Success criteria: narrative/flavor candidates generate and integrate on human selection; tone/soul remains a human decision; additive and provenance-tracked.

#### Milestone 60: Balance Tuning Co-Pilot and Release Readiness Go/No-Go Surface
Goal: bridge automated findings to human decisions, and consolidate all verdicts into one release sign-off.
Target deliverables: a balance tuning co-pilot that turns Milestone 50 findings into actionable recommendations a human approves/tweaks, then re-verifies; a consolidated release-readiness bundle (four gates + balance + fun-feel gate + compliance + Steam-export readiness) extending the Milestone 25/44 provenance; a read-only go/no-go sign-off surface for a human; a demo and Scenario Coverage v54.
Success criteria: balance recommendations are human-approved and re-verified; a single read-only surface shows complete release readiness; the human go/no-go is required and recorded; #1 and #23 remain open.

#### Milestone 61: Era J Roadmap and #1 Governance Refresh, and Creative-Leverage Assessment
Goal: record Era J completion and assess the residual human core.
Target deliverables: update the roadmap to record Milestones 57–60 as completed only after merged evidence; assess creative leverage (output per human decision) and explicitly document the permanent human core (fun verdict, taste, release go/no-go) and the external Ring-3 market boundary; a #1 completion comment; reaffirm all Era J boundaries.
Success criteria: the roadmap and #1 reflect actual Era J completion with evidence; the permanent human-judgment boundary and Ring-3 market boundary are reaffirmed; #1 and #23 remain open.
