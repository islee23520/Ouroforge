# Era J Creative-Leverage Assessment (Milestones 57-60)

Status: **Era J complete on merged evidence; descriptive governance assessment only.**

Era J tested whether Ouroforge can increase output per human creative/release decision while preserving the permanent human core: fun verdict, taste, tone/soul, human curation, and release go/no-go. The answer is bounded and descriptive: Ouroforge can multiply local proposal and evidence surfaces around a human decision, but it cannot replace the decision.

## Assessment

Era J improves **creative leverage** by making the work around each human decision reproducible, local, and inspectable:

- Candidate generation multiplies proposal breadth, but selected output remains human curation and later review/apply work.
- Playtest capture and fun-feel gating make human verdicts auditable, but do not compute or infer fun.
- Narrative assist expands candidate material and provenance, but does not judge tone, soul, humor, or taste.
- Balance co-pilot and release readiness gather mechanical evidence, but require human approval, human go/no-go, and external release authority.

The milestone therefore increases output per human decision only in the evidence/proposal layer. It does not claim automated creativity, automated quality, automated fun, autonomous shipping, production readiness, market demand, or Godot replacement/parity.

## Permanent human core

These decisions remain human-owned and out of trusted automation scope:

- Fun/feel verdict and taste.
- Tone, soul, humor, narrative fit, and creative direction.
- Candidate selection as curation.
- Balance recommendation approval and any later trusted application decision.
- Release go/no-go and public release authority.
- Steam account creation, code signing, content survey, store submission, release-button action, wishlists, UA, discoverability, and market demand.

## Boundary and remaining gaps

Rust/local owns trusted validation, evidence composition, provenance, persistence, run/project binding, review/apply/trust-gradient checks, and CLI behavior. Browser, dashboard, cockpit, and Studio surfaces remain read-only or draft/proposal-only for trusted state. Generated runs/assets/builds/artifacts remain untracked unless explicitly fixture-scoped. Hosted/cloud/mobile Layer-3 capability remains DEFER per #1508; distributed/Elixir remains NO-GO under ADR #92.

Remaining gaps are intentionally outside Era J: real-player market validation, commercial release operations, store/account/signing work, live balancing, hosted/cloud/mobile services, and any claim that a title is good, fun, shippable, production-ready, or marketable. #1 and #23 remain open governance anchors.
